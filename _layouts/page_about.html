Robotics Graduate Student at Carnegie Mellon University

Prior to grad school, I was a Research Engineer at IBM Research, working in the Collaborative Cognition group. I started as a member of the core team building the cognitive platform IBM Watson Recruitment (IWR) and later transitioned into extending Collaborative Decision Making to the cognitive recruitment ecosystem. In simple words, this can be thought of as an embodiment of decentralized artificial intelligence that allowed for multiple stakeholders/intelligent autonomous agents to share their private knowledge, beliefs, and intentions over a sequential privacy-preserving gameplay and collectively reach a consensus on which candidates to hire.

Someday I want to build truly adaptive intelligent systems which are capable of not just co-existing but co-evolving alongside us humans. While my technical research background has been scattered in statistical machine learning methods, geometric computer vision and image understanding, planning and decision making in multi-agent networks, natural language processing for information extraction; my philosophical research interests continue to revolve around the mysteries of human cognition and how their application in intelligent systems can enable them to better collaborate (understand, communicate, act) with us and eventually become entities that are welcomed as our everyday companions.

Like most people I am thoroughly entertained by science-fiction literature and drama around robotics/AI even though it might seem to be dark, bleak and apocalyptic. Even though a part of me would love to see this happen for the excitement and storyline such a situation would provide; when saner thoughts prevail my motivation behind pursuing the field is to enhance human capability. I believe there are two kinds of things we are not supposed to be doing (or at least not supposed to be doing alone) -

  • difficult things, which we are not physically/mentally capable of doing.
  • mundane things, which consume our time that could have been better spent on pursuits like creativity, ideation, relationships, politics and social good.
I see AI and robotics as evolutionary catalysts capable of solving fundamental things beyond automation - particularly breaking social-constructs that dictate the role an individual plays in society.
TL;DR: I want a robot to make a sandwich for me.
